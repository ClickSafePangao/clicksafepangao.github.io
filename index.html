What is the purpose of this website?
The aim of this website is to teach and empower people by offering credible information regarding online scams. We seek to make people aware of typical methods employed by scammers, provide practical tips on how to identify and avoid fraud, and assist users in safeguarding their personal and financial data. Types of Scams

Phishing


Phishing is a type of cyber attack in which a cybercriminal sends an unsolicited message to a user with the intent of stealing their personal information. Cybercriminals trick users into revealing their personal information such as their login credentials or credit card numbers by impersonating a familiar face or reputable business. Phishing scams send messages, such as emails or texts, with a malicious attachment or link that downloads malware on your device or directs you to a spoofed website. These spoofed websites imitate legitimate websites and try to trick you into revealing your personal information.

Catfishing


Catfishing is a type of social engineering attack in which a cybercriminal creates a fake online identity to deceive and exploit someone else. Most catfishing scams try to initiate a romantic relationship with a victim through social media or dating apps. Catfish scammers exploit those who are emotionally vulnerable to steal the victimâ€™s money or personal information.

Social Media Scam


Social media scams are online scams, conducted via social media platforms, in which scammers post fake promotions, send malicious links or impersonate online accounts to steal a userâ€™s personal information. Social media scams can come through a userâ€™s newsfeed or messages, where the scammer convinces the victim to click a malicious link or message back their personal information.

Online Shopping Scam


Online shopping scams are when scammers try to portray themselves as legitimate online sellers. Scammers will often create a fake website that impersonates a legitimate online retailer. Some scammers create fake accounts on online marketplaces to trick users into thinking they are legitimate sellers. They often leave fake online reviews to make it seem more legitimate. When a user tries to buy from the fake website, account or page, the scammer tricks the user into giving up their financial and personal information. These types of scammers will bait-and-switch the victim by giving them a counterfeit product or no product at all.

PREVENTION TIPS

1. Think Before You Click
Don't click on suspicious links in emails, texts, or social media messages. Hover over links to see the real URL before clicking.
2. Verify the Source
Always double-check emails or messages that ask for personal or financial info. Contact the company directly using trusted methods.
3. Use Strong, Unique Passwords
Don't reuse passwords across multiple sites. Use a password manager to keep track.
4. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
It adds an extra layer of security, even if your password gets stolen.
5. Keep Your Devices Updated
Regularly update your OS, browser, and apps to patch security vulnerabilities.
6. Be Cautious with Public Wi-Fi
Avoid entering sensitive information when using public Wi-Fi. Use a VPN if you need to connect to public networks.
7. Look for Secure Websites
Make sure sites use HTTPS (not just HTTP)â€”look for the padlock icon in the address bar.
8. Watch for Red Flags in Communication
Urgency, threats, or promises of easy money are classic scam tactics. Poor grammar or strange phrasing can be a giveaway.
9. Don't Overshare Online
Be careful about what personal info you postâ€”scammers can use it for social engineering.
10. Educate Yourself and Others
Stay up to date on common scams. Help friends and family recognize potential fraud, especially those who may be more vulnerable.
